Italy! Germany! Slovenia! Mexico! Azerbaijan! Everywhere you looked during the 39th Rhythmic Gymnastics World Championships, gymnasts were recording new all-time best results, rewriting the sport’s history books. That the Mediterranean wind that whirled through Sofia’s (BUL) Arena Armeec last week was generated by a young woman also called Sofia is one of the quirks of a World Championships that will go down as one of the most exciting ever. With six of a possible nine golds, Italian gymnasts led by superstar Sofia Raffaeli (ITA) were the dominant force of a World Championships unlike any that has come before. Raffaeli, the most successful gymnast of the World Cup series in 2022, lived up to her pre-competition hype, winning titles with the Hoop, Ball, and Ribbon, then capturing the All-Around.

Bulgaria and Italy each added a World title to their impressive medal hauls as the Rhythmic Gymnastics World Championships drew to a close Sunday in Sofia (BUL). Two Group finals — with the 5 Hoops and the 3 Ribbons/2 Balls — took place as the curtain fell on the 39th edition of the World Championships, with familiar faces mounting the top steps of the podium in both events. Italy’s “Farfalle,” on the rebound from their disappointing fourth-place All-Around finish Friday, stormed the carpet with a hard-driving performance to “They Don’t Really Care About Us” for 34.950 points for gold with the 5 Hoops. The Bulgarians returned to the carpet after their own disappointment with the 5 Hoops to end the championships with the gold medal for their 3 Ribbons/2 Balls routine, a performance that mixed Bulgarian folklore with contemporary elements.
